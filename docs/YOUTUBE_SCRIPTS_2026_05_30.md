```
Master the periodization:
  - Period 1: 1491-1607 (often skipped — lowest weight, skip last)
  - Periods 3, 4, 5: 1754-1877 (heavy MCQ weight)
  - Period 7: 1890-1945 (heavy DBQ + LEQ weight)
```

## Script 5 — AP Calculus AB

```
0:00 [HOOK]
AP Calc AB has a 58% pass rate. The 5-rate is 22% — higher than most
APs because the people who take it are math-strong. Here's how to
prep efficiently.

0:15 [EXAM STRUCTURE]
- 45 MCQ (1 hr 45 min) — 30 no-calc + 15 calc
- 6 FRQ (1 hr 30 min) — 2 calc + 4 no-calc

Pass rate: ~58%. 5-rate: ~22%.

1:30 [HONEST DIFFICULTY]
The content is HARD but the AP exam is FAIR. CB tests the same
question types every year — derivatives, integrals, related rates,
optimization, volume of revolution.

If your precalc is solid, 4 weeks of prep gets you a 4. The 5 needs
mock practice and accuracy under time pressure.

3:00 [3-WEEK PLAN]
Week 1: Khan Academy AP Calc AB (free). Watch units 1-8 plus FRQ
strategy videos.
Week 2: Drill 50 problems per day. Half no-calculator (algebra is
your enemy), half with Desmos.
Week 3: 3 timed mocks. Review every wrong answer before next mock.

4:00 [INSIDER TIP]
The 6 FRQs are predictable:
  FRQ 1: Calculator, particle motion
  FRQ 2: Calculator, contextual application
  FRQ 3-6: No calc, varying topics

Always start with FRQ 1 — easiest points. Save the hardest for last
even if it means leaving it incomplete.

4:45 [CTA]
```
