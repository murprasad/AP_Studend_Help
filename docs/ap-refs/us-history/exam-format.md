## Section I, Part A — Multiple Choice (PrepLion Practice scope)
- **55 questions · 55 minutes · 40% of total score**
- Questions presented in **sets of 3-4** tied to a **stimulus**: primary/secondary source, image, graph, map, political cartoon
- Each MCQ = 1 raw point (55 raw total)
- **Format requirement for question generation:**
  - Every MCQ belongs to a set with a shared stimulus (2-4 Qs per set)
  - Stimulus types: primary-source excerpt (letter, speech, treaty), secondary-source excerpt (historian's argument), image, political cartoon, data table/graph, map
  - Each Q tests a different reasoning process against the same stimulus

## Section I, Part B — Short Answer (out of scope for MCQ generation; informational)
- 3 of 4 questions · 40 minutes · 20%
- Q1 + Q2 required; Q3 or Q4 student choice

## Section II — DBQ + Long Essay (out of scope)
- DBQ: 60 min · 25% · 7 documents · coverage 1754-1980
- Long Essay: 40 min · 15% · 3 options spanning different periods

## Historical Thinking Skills (assessed across all MCQs)
1. **Developments & Processes** — identify historical concepts, developments, processes
2. **Sourcing & Situation** — analyze point of view, context, audience of primary/secondary sources
3. **Claims & Evidence** — evaluate arguments in secondary sources
4. **Contextualization** — situate events in broader context
5. **Making Connections** — compare and causal-link
6. **Argumentation** — construct evidence-based arguments (essays only)

## Three Reasoning Processes (explicit on MCQ tags)
- **Comparison** — similarities/differences between events, periods, groups
- **Causation** — cause-effect relationships
- **Continuity & Change** — what persists vs what transforms

## Quality Rubric for Generated MCQs
Reference this when generating AP US History questions:

1. **Stimulus-anchored**: MCQ must reference a quoted primary/secondary source, image description, or data point. No "floating" context-free questions.
2. **Reasoning-process tagged**: Each Q maps to ONE of {comparison, causation, continuity-change}.
3. **Period-locked**: Q stimulus + correct answer must fit within the unit's specified time period (see `unit-weights.md`).
4. **Four-option MCQ (A-D)**: AP US History uses 4 choices, not 5.
5. **Distractor quality**: All distractors must be plausible period-appropriate options; no anachronisms, no obvious red herrings.
6. **No direct-recall bait**: avoid "What year did X happen?" — test reasoning, not memorization.
